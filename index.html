Red deer

Red deer are widely depicted in cave art found throughout European caves, with some of the artwork dating from as early as 40,000 years ago, during the Upper Paleolithic. The deer is the spirit animal of the gemini sign.

  • Scientific name: Cervus elaphus
  • Average weight: 200 kg
  • Average lifespan: 15 years
  • Habitat: Temperate forest

The red deer is the fourth-largest deer species behind moose, elk and sambar deer. It is a ruminant, eating its food in two stages and having an even number of toes on each hoof, like camels, goats and cattle.
